## What WFI does

- Produces evidence-based, quantitative welfare estimates grounded in peer-reviewed science.
- Provides transparent welfare metrics with explicit assumptions and uncertainty bounds.
- Offers structured comparisons across species, production systems, and conditions.
- Documents the provenance of all estimates, including data sources, model assumptions,
  and known limitations.
- Maintains a canonical methodology (WFF) subject to scientific revision.

---

## What WFI does not do

- WFI does not issue institutional moral or policy verdicts.
- WFI does not advocate for specific interventions or producers on behalf of the institution.
- WFI does not present estimates with false precision or suppress uncertainty.
- WFI does not treat contested scientific questions as settled in its public outputs.

---

## Relationship between evidence and advocacy

WFI's scientific outputs are designed to be useful to advocates, policymakers,
producers, funders, and others. The scientific canon does not prescribe specific
policy conclusions. It provides evidence, metrics, assumptions, and uncertainty
estimates that can inform decision-making. The welfare metrics produced by WFI
are inputs to decision-making, not decisions themselves.
