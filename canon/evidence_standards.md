## Purpose

This file defines the standards WFI applies when evaluating scientific evidence
for inclusion in canonical estimates, module inputs, and welfare assessments.

---

## Evidence hierarchy

[PLACEHOLDER — authoritative evidence hierarchy requires scientific review]

A tiered evidence hierarchy, adapted to the specific demands of affective
quantification and epidemiological review in animal welfare science, is to be
documented here following review. Provisional tiers likely include:

1. Systematic reviews and meta-analyses with explicit search criteria
2. Controlled experimental studies
3. Observational cohort studies
4. Cross-sectional and surveillance data
5. Expert elicitation (where empirical data are absent)

Do not treat this provisional list as the authoritative hierarchy.

## Publication bias and data gaps

WFF assessments must note where available evidence is likely to be affected
by publication bias, industry funding, or geographic concentration of studies.
Data gaps are to be documented explicitly and not silently filled with assumptions.

---

## Species coverage

Evidence standards may need to be applied differently across species, given
unequal research coverage. Where evidence is extrapolated across species,
the extrapolation must be documented, justified, and its uncertainty characterised.
See Module Ψ (`modules/psi_interspecific_scaling.md`).
