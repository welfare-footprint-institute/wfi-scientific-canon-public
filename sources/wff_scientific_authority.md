# WFF Scientific Authority and Amendment Rule

**Status:** Active governance rule  
**Last reviewed:** 2026-06-22  
**Scientific authorities:** Wladimir J. Alonso and Cynthia Schuck-Paim, acting jointly

## Purpose

This file defines the hierarchy of scientific authority for the Welfare Footprint Framework (WFF) and prevents public repository governance from being confused with authority to revise the framework itself.

## Authority hierarchy

### 1. Primary WFF methodological paper

The ultimate current scientific authority for the WFF is the primary methodological paper:

> Alonso, W. J., & Schuck-Paim, C. (2025). *Welfare Footprint Framework: Methodological Foundations and Quantitative Assessment Guidelines.* Center for Welfare Metrics. https://doi.org/10.17605/osf.io/94bxs

Until a peer-reviewed journal version supersedes the OSF preprint, the OSF version is the controlling methodological source.

### 2. Formally adopted amendments or addenda

A later amendment, addendum, correction, or clarification may supplement or revise the primary paper only when it is:

- explicitly identified as an amendment or addendum to the WFF;
- supported by a documented scientific rationale;
- jointly approved by Wladimir J. Alonso and Cynthia Schuck-Paim;
- recorded with a date, scope, affected concepts, and relationship to the primary paper;
- incorporated into the relevant scientific publication, formal amendment record, or equivalent controlled scientific document.

Neither author acting alone may unilaterally alter the foundational scientific content of the WFF.

If Wladimir and Cynthia do not agree, the proposed change remains unresolved and must not be represented as part of the WFF.

### 3. Internal scientific canon repository

The private scientific canon repository is the controlled, versioned operational representation of the WFF paper and any formally adopted amendments.

It does not independently create scientific authority.

### 4. Public scientific canon mirror

This public repository is a curated mirror of the internal canon and a public teaching, citation, review, and retrieval interface.

It must not precede or create a scientific amendment. Any substantive change must first be approved under the rule above and represented in the internal canon before it is propagated publicly.

### 5. Downstream tools and applications

Custom GPTs, connectors, WFKG, interactive interfaces, the Atlas, and other applications consume the WFF paper, formal amendments, and controlled canon representations.

They do not define or revise the WFF.

## Review versus scientific authority

Human review of a public repository change is necessary for quality control, but it is not sufficient to revise the WFF.

Repository review asks questions such as:

- Does this public file faithfully represent the WFF paper, adopted amendments, and approved internal canon?
- Is the terminology correct and consistent?
- Are provenance, status, and uncertainty represented accurately?
- Does the change preserve established WFF distinctions?

Scientific amendment asks a different question:

- Should the WFF itself change?

That question belongs to the higher-level scientific process described above and requires joint approval by Wladimir and Cynthia.

## Amendment record requirements

Any future formal amendment should record at minimum:

- amendment identifier and title;
- date;
- concepts or sections affected;
- scientific rationale and supporting evidence;
- whether it clarifies, supplements, corrects, or revises the primary paper;
- explicit joint approval by Wladimir and Cynthia;
- affected canon files and downstream artifacts;
- publication or public-release status.

## Conflict rule

If a source, public file, tool, or contributor proposal conflicts with the primary WFF paper or a formally adopted amendment:

1. do not silently change the WFF;
2. record the conflict and its provenance;
3. determine whether the problem is a public-mirror fidelity error, an internal canon fidelity error, a source-interpretation issue, or a genuine candidate for scientific amendment;
4. correct fidelity errors through the appropriate repository workflow;
5. route genuine amendment candidates to the joint scientific process;
6. leave unresolved disagreements explicitly unresolved.
