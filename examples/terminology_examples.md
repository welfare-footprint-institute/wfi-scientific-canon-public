# Terminology Examples

This file illustrates correct and incorrect use of WFF terminology,
particularly around the WFF chain, module names, and deprecated terms.

## Correct vs. incorrect usage

### The WFF chain

| Incorrect | Correct | Reason |
|-----------|---------|--------|
| "Welfare outcome: barren cage" | "Circumstance: barren cage" | A cage is a circumstance, not a welfare outcome |
| "Welfare metric: lesion score" | "Welfare indicator: lesion score" | Lesion scores are proxies, not affective metrics |
| "Pain from bone fracture is a biological consequence" | "Bone fracture is a biological consequence; pain is an affective experience" | Pain is affective, not biological |

---

### Module V terminology

| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| "Aggregation and Standardization (Module V)" | "Econometric Calculation (Module V)" |
| "The aggregation step (Module V)…" | "The Econometric Calculation (Module V)…" |

### Tool naming

| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| "Pain-Track GPT" | "Hedonic-Track GPT" |

---

### Valence and disaggregation

| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| "Total welfare hours: 120" | "Cumulative Pain (Category 1): 80 h; (Category 2): 35 h; (Category 3): 5 h; Cumulative Pleasure (Category X): [value]" |
| "Net welfare = pleasure − pain" | Cumulative Pain and Cumulative Pleasure are reported separately; any aggregation requires explicit justification |
