# Known Failure Modes

This file documents patterns of analytical error that WFF practitioners and
agents should actively avoid. Entries are drawn from methodology review and
should be referenced when reviewing new assessments.

## Failure Mode 1: Chain conflation

**Description:** Collapsing two levels of the WFF chain into one — most commonly
treating a Biological Consequence as if it were an Affective Experience.

**Example:** Reporting a footpad lesion score as a welfare metric without
bridging from the biological observation to the affective experience
(pain intensity, duration) it produces.

**Why it matters:** The welfare-relevant quantity is the affective experience.
Stopping at the biological level systematically understates welfare impact for
conditions with high affective intensity.

**Module boundary:** Belongs in Module II → Module III transition.

---

## Failure Mode 2: Valence collapse

**Description:** Summing Cumulative Pain and Cumulative Pleasure into a single
welfare score without explicit justification of the aggregation logic.

**Example:** Reporting "net welfare score = pleasure − pain" as if this were
a principled quantity, without stating what weighting or commensuration
assumption underlies the subtraction.

**Why it matters:** The relationship between positive and negative affect
is not simply additive. Collapsing valences obscures fundamentally different
kinds of welfare impact.

**Module boundary:** Belongs in Module V and Module VI.

---

## Failure Mode 3: Intensity category collapse

**Description:** Reporting a single "total pain hours" figure by summing across
intensity categories without disaggregating.

**Example:** Adding Category 1 (Annoying) hours and Category 4 (Excruciating)
hours as if they were equivalent units.

**Why it matters:** An hour of excruciating pain is not equivalent to an hour
of mild annoyance. Collapsing categories before applying a weighting scheme
destroys information critical to welfare assessment.

**Module boundary:** Belongs in Module V (aggregation must preserve categories
in primary outputs).

---

## Failure Mode 4: Treating absence of evidence as evidence of absence

**Description:** Assuming a condition causes no affective experience because no
study has directly measured the affective component.

**Example:** Treating respiratory disease in poultry as welfare-neutral because
studies characterise it in clinical terms without explicitly measuring distress.

**Why it matters:** The lack of affective measurement does not mean absence of
affective experience. The appropriate response is to note uncertainty, not to
assign zero.

**Module boundary:** Belongs in Modules III and IV.

---

## Failure Mode 5: Using industry categories as welfare descriptors

**Description:** Treating a commercial or regulatory system classification
(e.g., "free-range," "enriched colony") as if it fully specifies welfare-relevant
circumstances.

**Example:** Assuming all "enriched colony" cage systems provide equivalent
circumstances because they share a regulatory label.

**Why it matters:** System labels aggregate over substantial variation in
actual conditions. Module I must specify conditions at a level of detail
sufficient to support Module II inference.

**Module boundary:** Belongs in Module I.
